2GIS

Built the method behind effective creative.

2GIS had a huge product and strong usage, but brand and creative work still depended too much on separate tasks, subjective discussions and one-off wins. I built a clearer operating model: audience insight, positioning, message and visual direction, creative testing, launch measurement and in-house creative capability.

Company2GIS
RoleBrand Manager
Period2024-current
Scale70M+ users

Problem

At 70M-user scale, the company needed a repeatable way to make creative decisions: what we say, how we test it, how we launch, how we measure and how we improve the next iteration.

The same method had to work across product launches, regional teams, social, in-product stories, video, advertising, special projects and senior stakeholders.

My role

  • Built an evidence-led process for brand, creative and campaign decisions.
  • Hired and shaped the first in-house creative capability: two creators and a strategist around a clear brief-to-launch workflow.
  • Drove alignment across CEO, CMO, CPO, product leaders, senior marketing managers and commercial leadership.
  • Led by credibility rather than formal reporting lines: design, analytics, editorial, product and performance sat in adjacent teams.

What changed

  • Built the first brand platform and turned it into a practical decision system.
  • Created a tone of voice around real employees telling their own stories, making the brand more human and specific.
  • Relaunched Brand Health Tracking and split it into overall and city-level measurement.
  • Made creative testing mandatory before launch, including animatics before production and realistic 5-10 second exposure conditions.
  • Ran a visual-equity study, protected the ownable color combination and drove the redesign / new app icon program after more than a decade on the old system.
  • Reframed 2GIS as a multi-service mega-brand: search, navigation, discovery, social geolocation, recommendations and content.

Result

The brand team moved from reactive production support to a group other teams proactively consult. Campaigns became easier to brief, test, measure and align. Strategic choices moved from opinion battles into audience insight, creative tests, campaign data and brand-health measurement.

The campaign idea was "A reason to open 2GIS" - both a slogan and a CTA. For people used to another map service, the campaign sold the product through concrete possibilities: there are many city moments where opening 2GIS is worth trying.

Spontaneous brand awareness rose from 28% to 42% (+14 pp; about +50% relative). Top-of-mind awareness rose from 18% to 28% (+10 pp; about +56% relative). The measurement was taken two months after the campaign ended, which makes the result stronger than a short-lived launch-week spike.

Special projects extended the same perception work into physical city presence: Afisha Picnic in two major cities and a large summer city program with zones where people could play table tennis and spend time with friends.

For an organic special project around the many ways people name 2GIS - "DubльGIS", "DvaGIS", "TuGIS" - we placed three large media facades at a major interchange and launched an Instagram Reel built on a chat-argument trend. With no paid promotion, the Reel reached 816K views and 3K+ comments, compared with a typical ~5K views baseline for Reels.
