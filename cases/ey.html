EY

Digital communications for a 1,000+ store chain.

Embedded full-time at EY through the Pichesky partnership, I owned the digital communications part of a larger marketing strategy: budget analysis, customer research and a CRM-to-paid-media activation system. This is kept compact as consulting-style proof.

Client contextLarge jewelry retail chain
RoleInvited Digital Strategist
Period2021, 4 months
OutcomeC-level / shareholder approval

Work

The task was not a campaign execution brief. I structured the digital communications work as a consulting case: diagnose the commercial system, isolate the budget leakage, define the customer logic and turn it into a launch-ready operating model.

01 diagnoseBusiness system

Mapped marketing structure, budgets, channel routines, CRM data quality and in-store behavior through observation, interviews and quantitative research.

02 isolateBudget leakage

Found that close to half of marketing budget went into mostly generic SMS communication, with weak personalization and weak learning capture.

03 defineCustomer truth

Jewellery was often a spontaneous self-reward purchase: women buying pieces for themselves, not only receiving them as gifts.

04 buildOperating model

Converted phone and loyalty data into segments, custom audiences and lookalike logic for more relevant collection-led digital communication.

RecommendationReallocate SMS-heavy spend into a segmented brand-performance system.

The proposed model connected contact data, creative exposure, target action and repeat behavior, so campaign learnings would return to the next cycle instead of disappearing after each send-out.

Results

The strategy was approved at C-level and shareholder level because it connected three things senior stakeholders could act on: a large inefficient budget line, validated customer insight and a practical activation model with attribution.

My contract ended at strategy handoff, so the launch was continued by the client / EY-side team after my involvement. This case only claims the strategy, approval and launch-ready system I directly owned.

Budget opportunityIdentified a large SMS-heavy spend that could be used more efficiently through segmented digital communication.
Audience insightBuilt the strategy around women buying jewellery for themselves as a spontaneous self-reward.
Activation modelCRM, loyalty data, custom audiences, lookalikes, creative routes and attribution loop.
ApprovalSigned off at C-level and shareholder level as part of the broader marketing strategy.
